italian food list
food description
Pizza Napoletana Pizza Napoletana is a classic Italian pizza hailing from Naples. It's known for its simple, high-quality ingredients: a thin, chewy crust made from just flour, water, yeast, and salt; San Marzano tomatoes; fresh mozzarella cheese; and a drizzle of olive oil
Polenta Polenta is a Northern Italian dish made from boiled cornmeal. It's typically a coarse grind, giving it a bit of chew compared to grits. Eaten hot and creamy like porridge, it can be a base for sauces or cheeses. Leftovers can be cooled and solidified, then sliced and fried or grilled for another tasty meal.
Ribollita Ribollita is a hearty Tuscan soup made with cannellini beans, vegetables, and stale bread. It translates to "reboiled" because traditionally it was a way to use leftover vegetable soup by simmering it again with chunks of bread for a thicker, more substantial meal. Packed with flavor and texture, it's a delicious and frugal peasant dish.
Bottarga Bottarga is a Mediterranean delicacy made from cured fish roe. Think of it as the "sea truffle." Typically made from mullet or bluefin tuna, the roe sacs are salted, dried, and pressed concentrating their savory, umami flavor. It's most often grated over pasta, seafood, or vegetables for a salty, rich punch.
Canederli Canederli are savory bread dumplings from northern Italy, particularly Trentino-Alto Adige. Made with soaked stale bread, eggs, and herbs, they're often filled with cheese, speck (smoked pork), or sausage. Canederli are boiled in broth for a comforting and hearty dish, or pan-fried for a crispier texture. They're a delicious example of "cucina povera" - frugal cooking that uses simple ingredients to create a satisfying meal.
